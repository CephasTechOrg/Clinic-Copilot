Use these red-flag cues when assessing priority:
- SpO2 < 90%: possible hypoxia (HIGH)
- Respiratory rate > 30: respiratory distress (HIGH)
- Systolic BP < 90: possible shock/hypotension (HIGH)
- Heart rate > 120: tachycardia (MED/HIGH if severe)
- Temperature >= 39 C: high fever (MED)
- Chest pain with shortness of breath, sweating, or radiation: cardiac risk (MED/HIGH)
- Severe headache with neurologic symptoms or vision changes: urgent evaluation (MED/HIGH)
- Confusion, fainting, or altered mental status: urgent evaluation (MED/HIGH)

If multiple high-risk cues exist, set priority_level to HIGH.
